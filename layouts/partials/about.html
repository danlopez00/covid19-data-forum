More about the event

The initial response to the COVID-19 pandemic from the Community has been astounding. Epidemiologists, public health experts, statisticians, data scientists and researchers with R expertise have published a large number of predictive models, interactive resource allocation applications, and disease tracking dashboards. This effort has been successful in making data from reliable sources readily available by providing code to access and process the data for analysis.

However, most of this work has been done by individuals and small groups working in isolation without the benefit of standards or a common framework for analysis. Additionally, most of the contributed models and analyses were necessarily opportunistic in the sense that they were built with the data at hand and with limited opportunity for guidance from and with limited opportunity for guidance from the decision makers responsible for public health policy.

We are now entering a new phase in the analytical effort to combat the disease caused by the novel corona virus. The alarm bell has been rung and heard. To continue in an uncoordinated fashion would be counterproductive. We believe that now is the time to initiate a cooperative effort to design and develop a small number of high quality, trusted tools to assist public officials and health care administrators in making good decisions.

The intent of the summit is to focus on the data required to build actionable models to predict the likely evolution of the pandemic. We hope to identify gaps in the kinds of data required and to reach agreement on the features that predictive models ought to possess in order to assist authorities charged with making real-time decisions during the next phase of the pandemic. We also hope to develop guidelines to assist developers and modelers who are building open source tools for modeling and other aids to planning and decision making. These goals are potentially useful now, with COVID-19, and are essential for the future pandemics that are sure to come.

The COVID-19 Data Forum will be a series of online meetings with both a public set of talks as well as smaller invited expert workshop for topic experts to focus on various aspects of the modeling process.
